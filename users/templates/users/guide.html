Account Setup

Welcome to DCC — Dinau Control Center, Papua New Guinea's centralised credit bureau platform. This guide walks you through every feature so you can get maximum value from your subscription.

Completing Your Profile

Before you can use most features, complete your organisation profile at Users → Profile. You'll need:

  • Organisation name — displayed on records you submit
  • IPA Number and TIN — required for verification
  • Work email — used for billing and system notifications
  • LUID — your unique Lender ID assigned by DCC (contact info@dc.com.pg if not assigned)
Tip

LoanMasta users: your account is automatically synced. Non-LoanMasta users need to complete profile setup manually before data submissions are accepted.

Theme & Settings

Switch between dark and light theme at Users → Settings. You can also configure credit check window hours (how long a paid access window stays open after unlocking a credit file).

Your Dashboard

The Dashboard at /users/dashboard/ is your command centre. It shows:

Defaults Summary

Your portfolio's current arrears (K amount), defaults count, and loans in recovery — each links to a filtered list.

Today's Activity

Records you created or updated today, with percentage change vs. yesterday.

DCC Network Activity

New records across all DCC institutions today — gives you a sense of the network's growth.

Quick Search

Search borrower names directly from the dashboard — returns matching client records instantly.

Adding Client Records

You can add individual client records at My Records → Add Record.

Required Fields

  • Full Name (First, Middle, Last)
  • Date of Birth
  • NID Number — used for deduplication and matching across lenders
  • Mobile Number
  • Loan Reference
  • Loan Amount and Status
Tip — NID Matching

When you enter a client's NID number, DCC automatically checks if that person exists in other lenders' records. If matched, a CUID (Cross-institution Unique ID) is assigned, linking their history across all lenders. This is what makes the cross-lender credit check possible.

DCC Status Flags

Each client record carries a DCC Status. Key statuses:

  • CLEAR — No outstanding defaults
  • ARREARS — Behind on repayments
  • DEFAULT — Formally defaulted loan
  • RECOVERY — In active debt recovery
  • SETTLED — Debt resolved via settlement
  • BLACKLISTED — Barred from new credit

Adding Business Records

For business clients, use My Records → Add Business. Business records use the IPA registration number as the matching key instead of NID.

Bulk Upload

Upload hundreds of records at once using a spreadsheet file at My Records → Upload Records.

Upload Steps

1
Download the Template

Use the downloadable Excel template to ensure your columns are correctly labelled. Do not rename columns.

2
Enter Your Data

One row per client. Required: First Name, Last Name, NID, Mobile, Loan Ref, Amount, Status, Funding Date.

3
Enter Your LUID

Your Lender Unique ID (from your profile) must be entered before uploading. This identifies your institution as the data source.

4
Submit and Wait for Processing

Records are processed row by row. Errors are reported per-row. Successful records appear in your Client Records list immediately.

Non-LoanMasta Uploaders

If your account is not linked to LoanMasta, uploaded records go through a DCC verification workflow before going public. DCC will contact the borrower to verify the default claim. You'll be notified of the outcome.

Searching the Database

Search across all DCC member institution records at Database → Search.

How Matching Works

  • Search by name, NID, mobile, or email
  • Results show all clients matching your query across all lenders
  • DCC Status flag is always visible — no credit check needed to see if someone is flagged
  • Full cross-lender history requires unlocking the credit file (billed per view)

Public vs. Private Records

  • Your own records — always visible in full, no charge
  • Other lenders' records — name and status visible, full details require a paid credit check
  • Recent clients — the Recent Records page shows the latest additions across the network

Credit Checks

A credit check unlocks the full cross-lender credit file for a specific borrower. It is billed per view according to your plan.

Running a Credit Check

1
Find the Client

Search for the client in your records or the DCC database. Open their record detail page.

2
Unlock the Credit File

Click "View Data" on the locked credit check panel. Confirm the charge. Access is billed at your plan rate.

3
Review the Full Profile

You'll see the DCC Benchmark Score, all matched profiles across lenders, complete loan history, arrears, and outstanding balances.

4
Access Window

Your access stays open for the number of hours configured in your account settings (default: 12 hours). Within that window, re-visiting the page is free.

Downloading a Credit Report

While a credit file is unlocked, you can download a PDF credit report from the client detail page using the Download PDF Report link. The PDF includes all cross-lender loan history and the DCC Benchmark Score.

Watch List & Alerts

The Watch List lets you flag clients so that any change to their DCC record triggers an email alert to your account. This is especially useful for monitoring defaulted or high-risk clients.

Adding a Client to Watch List

1
Open the Client's Record

Navigate to any client detail page via My Records or the DCC database search.

2
Scroll to the Watch List Section

At the bottom of the client detail page, find the Watch List card.

3
Choose Alert Types and Save

Select which changes should trigger an alert: Status, Contact Details, Address, Employer, New Loan, or "Any Change". Add an optional note for your reference, then click Watch This Client.

What Triggers an Alert?

  • Status Change — DCC status or flag changes (e.g. CLEAR → DEFAULT)
  • Contact Details — Email or mobile number updated
  • Address — Residential or postal address changes
  • Employer — Employer or job details updated
  • New Loan — A new loan record is added for this client
  • Any Change — Alerts on all of the above
Use Case

A borrower defaults and disappears. Add them to your Watch List. The moment any lender updates their employer or address in DCC, you get an email with a direct link to their updated credit file — so you can follow up the recovery.

Managing Your Watch List

View all watched clients at Credit Actions → Watch List. Remove any client from the list using the Remove button on that page or on the client detail page.

Billing Note

Watch List alerts are a premium feature. Each alert email sent to your account is logged. Depending on your plan, high-volume alert usage may incur additional charges — check your billing page for current usage.

Portfolio Analytics

Portfolio Analytics at Credit Actions → Portfolio Analytics gives you a visual overview of your entire loan book's risk profile.

What's Available

  • DCC Status Distribution — Pie chart of Clear / Arrears / Default / Recovery / Settled across your records
  • Monthly Arrears Trend — Line chart showing arrears over time
  • Top Defaulters — Your highest-outstanding clients ranked by arrears amount
  • Recovery Progress — Loans in recovery and their outstanding balances
  • DCC Coverage — What percentage of your borrowers have DCC cross-lender data available

See the Recovery Insights page for a focused view of your clients in default/recovery stages with direct links to each client's record.

Default Notices

Issue formal default notices to borrowers directly from within DCC. All notices are logged with a timestamp and are part of the client's permanent DCC record.

Submitting a Default Notice

Navigate to a client's record detail page and use Actions → Submit Default Notice, or go to Credit Actions → Default Notices and choose a client.

Notice Types

  • Demand Notice — Formal demand for outstanding repayment
  • Default Notice — Official notification of loan default
  • Legal Referral Notice — Notice that matter has been referred for legal action
  • Settlement Offer — Formal offer of partial settlement

Notice Workflow

Once submitted, a notice enters the DCC workflow: PENDING → NOTIFIED → ACKNOWLEDGED → SETTLED / DISPUTED / CANCELLED. DCC staff manage escalation and updates on each notice.

Disputes

If a borrower disputes information in their credit file, or if your institution needs to raise a formal dispute about another lender's data, DCC manages this through a structured workflow.

Who Can Raise a Dispute?

  • Borrowers — via the Borrower Self-Service Portal
  • Your organisation — via the client detail page → Submit Dispute

Dispute Types

  • Incorrect personal details
  • Wrong loan amount or status
  • Fraudulent record (identity theft)
  • Data belongs to a different person
  • Loan already repaid / settled

Resolution Process

DCC reviews all disputes within 5 business days. Status tracks through: OPEN → UNDER REVIEW → RESOLVED / REJECTED. All parties are notified by email at each stage.

Debt Settlement Brokerage

DCC acts as a neutral broker to facilitate debt settlements between your institution and defaulted borrowers. All negotiations are documented, and the platform maintains a full communication audit trail.

How to Open a Settlement Case

Contact DCC at info@dc.com.pg or, if you have dashboard access, navigate to your client's record and request a settlement case through Actions → Request Debt Settlement.

Settlement Workflow

1
OPEN

DCC opens the case with lender and borrower details recorded.

2
NEGOTIATING

DCC contacts the borrower and facilitates message exchange between all parties. Lender and borrower can submit messages via the platform.

3
OFFER MADE

A formal settlement offer is drafted — specifying the agreed amount and payment timeline.

4
ACCEPTED / SETTLED

Once payment is confirmed, the case is marked SETTLED and the borrower's DCC status is updated accordingly.

Why Use DCC Brokerage?

DCC's involvement gives borrowers a trusted, neutral party to engage with — dramatically increasing engagement rates vs. direct lender contact. Settlement outcomes are documented and the borrower's DCC status reflects the resolution, incentivising their cooperation.

Borrower Self-Service Portal

The Borrower Portal at /borrower/ allows your borrowers to log in with their email and a PIN to:

  • View their own DCC credit summary and status
  • See who has accessed their credit file (enquiry log)
  • Submit disputes about incorrect data
  • Review and manage their consent history
Enable the Portal

The Borrower Portal is disabled by default. DCC SaaS Admin can enable it in Platform Settings → Feature Toggles. Once enabled, borrowers need a BorrowerAccount created by DCC staff to log in.

Benefits for Lenders

  • Reduces enquiries to your team — borrowers self-serve
  • Builds trust — borrowers can see exactly what's on their file
  • Dispute workflow integration — disputes raised via the portal flow directly to DCC's resolution queue

API Access

DCC provides a REST API for system-to-system integration. Use it to sync your Loan Management System (LMS) automatically, run credit checks programmatically, or build custom integrations.

Getting an API Key

API keys are issued by DCC Admin. Contact info@dc.com.pg to request access. Keys are scoped to specific permissions and rate limits.

Base URL

https://dc.com.pg/tpapi/v1/

Authentication

All API requests require a Bearer token in the Authorization header:

Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY

Key Endpoints

EndpointMethodDescription
/clients/search/GETSearch the DCC database
/clients/{id}/GETGet a specific client record
/clients/upload/POSTUpload client records
/credit-check/POSTRun a billable credit check
/loans/GET/POSTList or create loan records
LoanMasta Integration

If you use LoanMasta, your system may already be configured for automatic data sync via API. Check Settings → API / Integrations to verify your sync status and last sync timestamp.
